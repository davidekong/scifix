Mantis Shrimp

Did you know?

The Mantis shrimp has the most complex eyes in the animal kingdom, with up to 16 types of photoreceptors. They are also capable of seeing circular polarized light and multispectral images. Some species of mantis shrimp can even see ultraviolet and infrared light, which is outside of the visible spectrum for humans. The mantis shrimp's eyes are mounted on movable stalks, which allow them to independently focus on two different objects at the same time. They are also known for their powerful claws, which they use to attack and kill prey.

Bloodhound

Did you know?

The bloodhound is a breed of dog known for its keen sense of smell and ability to track scents over long distances. They have been used for centuries to track humans and animals, and are still used today by law enforcement agencies and search and rescue teams to locate missing people. Bloodhounds are bred specifically for their sense of smell, which is thought to be up to 100 times more sensitive than a human's. They have a long, drooping face and large nostrils that help them draw in more air and pick up on faint scents. They are also able to follow a scent trail that is days or even weeks old, and can even track a person's scent through water and across a variety of terrain. In addition to their exceptional sense of smell, bloodhounds are also known for their gentle and patient personality.
